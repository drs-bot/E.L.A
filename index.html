Reported (Indirect) Speech

Tense Backshift Rules

Direct Speech→Reported Speech
Simple Present ("is")→Simple Past (was)
Simple Past ("said")→Past Perfect (had said)
Past Continuous ("was painting")→Past Perfect Continuous (had been painting)
Present Perfect ("has cleaned")→Past Perfect (had cleaned)
Past Perfect ("had written")→Past Perfect (stays the same)

⚠️ Also change: pronouns & time expressions

  • Pronouns: I → he/she, we → they, my → his/her
  • Time: now → then, today → that day, yesterday → the day before, in 1943 → in 1943 (specific year stays!)
  • Place: here → there, this → that
1. Old Major said, "Man is the only creature that consumes without producing."
Tip: "is" → was · "consumes/produces" → consumed/produced
Old Major said that man was the only creature that consumed without producing.
2. "My grandfather built this house in 1943."
Tip: Simple Past → Past Perfect · "my" → his/her · specific year stays
He/She said that his/her grandfather had built that house in 1943.
3. "Picasso was painting Guernica at that time."
Tip: Past Continuous → Past Perfect Continuous
He/She said that Picasso had been painting Guernica at that time.
4. "The cleaner has cleaned the office."
Tip: Present Perfect → Past Perfect
He/She said that the cleaner had cleaned the office.
5. "He had written three books before 1867."
Tip: Past Perfect stays Past Perfect
He/She said that he had written three books before 1867.

Literary Devices: Metaphor, Simile & Allegory

Simile

Compares two things using "like" or "as".

"Grief feels like an endless ribbon."
→ uses like
Metaphor

Says one thing is another — no "like" or "as".

"Life is a journey."
→ direct comparison
Allegory

An entire story where characters & events represent real-world ideas or historical figures.

Animal Farm = allegory of the Russian Revolution

Quick trick to tell them apart

Ask yourself: is it one sentence (simile/metaphor) or an entire story with hidden meaning (allegory)? Then check for "like/as" to decide between simile and metaphor.

In Belzhar, Jam says grief feels like "an endless ribbon." What does this metaphor help us understand?
Wait — is this a metaphor or a simile? Notice the word "like"!
Correct answer: C — She feels like grief is something long that you never know when it will end.

Note: The sentence actually uses "like", which makes it a simile, not a metaphor — but the question tests your understanding of the image it creates: grief as something without end or clear boundary.

Show, Don't Tell

Instead of telling us how a character feels, show it through actions, senses, and details.

❌ Tell
Jam misses her home, her family, and especially Reeve.
✅ Show
At night, Jam stared at the ceiling, tracing the cracks the way she once traced the map of her street back home. She picked up her phone, then set it down. There was no one to call. Reeve's name sat in her contacts like a door she couldn't open.

Propaganda

Definition

Propaganda is information — often biased or misleading — used to promote a particular political cause, leader, or ideology. It aims to influence people's opinions and actions, usually without them realising they are being manipulated.

Common propaganda techniques

  • Repetition: Repeating slogans until people believe them ("Four legs good, two legs bad!")
  • Fear: Threatening terrible consequences if you don't obey
  • Half-truths: Mixing facts with lies to seem credible
  • Scapegoating: Blaming problems on a single enemy (Snowball)
  • Rewriting history: Changing facts of the past to suit the present
Squealer is Napoleon's spokesperson. Why is propaganda used, and how does it keep the animals under control?
Napoleon uses propaganda through Squealer to maintain power because he cannot rule by force alone — the animals outnumber the pigs. Propaganda works by making the animals doubt their own memories and believe that Napoleon's decisions are always right.

Example 1: When the pigs take the milk and apples, Squealer claims it is for "brain-workers" and that without the pigs, Jones would return. Fear stops the animals from questioning this.

Example 2: Squealer constantly rewrites history — he convinces the animals that Snowball was always a traitor and that the windmill was Napoleon's idea all along. Without written records, the animals can't prove otherwise.

Propaganda keeps the animals passive, confused, and afraid to rebel.

Animal Farm — Historical Counterparts

George Orwell wrote Animal Farm as an allegory of the Russian Revolution (1917) and the rise of Stalinist USSR. Every major character represents a real figure.

Character in the book Real historical figure Role
Old Major 🐷 Karl Marx / Vladimir Lenin Inspired the revolution with his dream; died before it happened
Napoleon 🐷 Joseph Stalin Ruthless leader who seized power, used force and propaganda
Snowball 🐷 Leon Trotsky Idealistic leader, eventually exiled and blamed for everything
Squealer 🐷 Soviet propaganda machine / Vyacheslav Molotov Spreads propaganda and justifies Napoleon's decisions
Boxer 🐴 The working class Loyal, hardworking, ultimately exploited and discarded
Mr. Jones 👨‍🌾 Tsar Nicholas II The old oppressive ruler overthrown by the revolution

Key exam tip

When asked about "counterparts," the question is asking which real person each Animal Farm character represents. Always name the character AND the historical figure.

Belzhar — Mental Health & Emotional Healing

About the book

Belzhar by Meg Wolitzer follows Jam Gallahue, a teenager grieving the loss of Reeve, a boy she fell in love with. She is sent to The Wooden Barn, a therapeutic boarding school. Through a special journal, she re-enters her past with Reeve — a space called "Belzhar."

Mental Health theme

The school recognises that grief, trauma, and emotional pain require real care — not just time. Each student has a unique wound: loss, abuse, anxiety, identity.

Emotional Healing theme

Healing happens through community, honesty, and confronting the truth. The journal forces characters to face their past rather than hide from it.

How does Belzhar explore mental health and emotional healing? Give examples.
Belzhar explores mental health by showing that grief is not just sadness — it can be paralyzing and isolating. Jam describes grief as "an endless ribbon," a simile that captures how her pain feels without a clear end in sight.

The school itself is a symbol of the idea that mental health needs structured support. Students are not "fixed" by being there; instead, they are given space and community. Jam's friendship with her classmates — each carrying their own trauma — shows that healing is easier when you feel understood.

The journal (Belzhar) is central to emotional healing: by re-living the past, the characters must eventually face the truth they have been avoiding. True healing begins only when Jam accepts what really happened with Reeve, not the version she wanted to believe.

Verb Tenses: Simple Past, Present Perfect & Past Perfect

Tense When to use Signal words Example
Simple Past Completed action at a specific past time yesterday, last night, in [year], then, suddenly She saved the file last night.
Present Perfect Past action connected to now; life experiences; recent results since, for, already, yet, so far, ever, never, this month I have worked here for three years.
Past Perfect Action that happened BEFORE another past action ("past of the past") by the time, already, before, after, when, had By 9 AM, my team had already prepared everything.

Story exercise — answer key

  1. I have worked (Present Perfect — "for three years now" = ongoing connection to present)
  2. had already prepared (Past Perfect — happened before the clock struck 9 AM)
  3. turned (Simple Past — specific past action)
  4. had forgotten (Past Perfect — realised AFTER the crash; happened before)
  5. had never felt (Past Perfect — "up to that moment" = before that past point)
  6. looked (Simple Past — specific action in the story)
  7. had already downloaded (Past Perfect — she did it before she left = earlier past)
  8. has implemented (Present Perfect — started in past, effect continues now)
  9. have not made (Present Perfect — "since that day" = ongoing)
  10. have gone (Present Perfect — "so far this month" = ongoing)
